Coca-Cola

Global organizations, including the media sector, often lack clarity in job expectations, leading to subjective appraisals and dissatisfaction. To address this, we adopted Gallup Q12's approach, emphasizing clear job expectations, and introduced data-driven Key Result Areas (KRAs). A comprehensive Job Directory was also developed, outlining roles and expectations, while benchmarking studies were created to inform hiring decisions.

More

The problem with many organizations worldwide is that the expected outcome of a job one is doing is not clearly defined. Result is that one ends up being appraised on something at the end of the year of which neither he was made aware of in the beginning of the year nor during the entire year worst is that most of the time the appraisal is on many subjective KRAs which are extremely difficult to measure. Media being no exception, had similar kind of issues where the managers rating their subordinates on either subjective KRAs or no clear defined KRAs leaving enough ambiguity and heartburn thereafter. We embarked upon the journey of Gallup Q12 first Question which states 'I know what is expected of me at work' and put Objectivity in KRAs of all the roles resulting into something which can be measured and is data based. The role holder himself knows his performance clearly during the entire year thereby relieving him of a false sense of achievement. It becomes easier for manager also to do performance appraisal since data speaks for itself. This helps in a better expectation management during appraisals and less heartburns during and after the process. Subsequently a Job Directory was created where each role, its purpose, Job Family and KRAs are mentioned which acts as a bible for recruiters and HR team across the organization. Parallely a Manpower and Salary Benchmarking study for a National Hindi News Channel and a Regional Channel was also created for Times to be used for all hiring decisions.
