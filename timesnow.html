Times Now: Clarity and Objectivity in Performance Evaluation

Times Now is a prominent Indian television news channel. It is owned and operated by The Times Group, one of India's largest media conglomerates. Times Now is known for its 24/7 news coverage, which includes national and international news, politics, business, sports, entertainment, and more. Times Now has gained recognition for its in-depth reporting, debates, and discussions on various issues of public interest. It has also been known to conduct high-profile interviews with politicians, celebrities, and influential figures.


In the world of media, as in many other industries, the lack of clearly defined expectations for job roles can lead to a myriad of challenges. Employees often find themselves being appraised at the end of the year for criteria they were not made aware of at the beginning of the year, or worse yet, evaluated on subjective Key Result Areas (KRAs) that are difficult to quantify. Times Now, a prominent Indian television news channel, was no exception to these issues. Managers were often left with the daunting task of rating their team members based on ambiguous or ill-defined performance criteria, leading to frustration and uncertainty among employees. Times Now embarked on a transformative journey by adopting the Gallup Q12's first question, which revolves around the statement: 'I know what is expected of me at work.' This foundational principle aimed to bring clarity and objectivity to KRAs for all roles within the organization. The result was a shift towards measurable and data-driven performance metrics, empowering employees to understand their performance throughout the year, and alleviating any false sense of achievement. This data-driven approach not only made performance appraisal more straightforward for managers but also facilitated better expectation management during the appraisal process, reducing post-appraisal heartburn.

More

Furthermore, Times Now introduced a comprehensive Job Directory, detailing each role, its purpose, Job Family, and KRAs. This directory has become a crucial resource for recruiters and the HR team, serving as a guiding reference for hiring decisions across the organization. In parallel, Times Now conducted a Manpower and Salary Benchmarking study for a National Hindi News Channel and a Regional Channel. This benchmarking data has since played a pivotal role in informing and guiding Times Now's hiring decisions, ensuring that the organization remains competitive and aligned with industry standards. Times Now's commitment to clarity, objectivity, and data-driven decision-making in performance evaluation and hiring processes has not only enhanced the work experience for its employees but also positioned the channel as a leader in the ever-evolving media landscape. This proactive approach reflects Times Now's dedication to fostering a culture of transparency, fairness, and excellence within its ranks.
