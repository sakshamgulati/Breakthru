Bharti Airtel: Elevating Customer Satisfaction through Innovation

Airtel is one of India's largest and most prominent telecommunications companies. With a vast customer base numbering in the millions, Airtel has always prioritized customer satisfaction as a critical aspect of its business operations. To gauge and enhance customer satisfaction, Airtel regularly measured its Customer Satisfaction (CSat) scores, a process facilitated by the Indian Marketing Research Bureau (IMBR), an independent third-party vendor. These scores aimed to assess customer contentment at various touchpoints, including the Call Center and Airtel Relationship Centers (ARCs) spread across India, which served as retail outlets for both sales and customer issue resolution.

However, a significant challenge surfaced in the operation of ARCs. These centers were managed by business partners who hired customer service staff on their payroll. Limited financial resources led to high staff attrition rates, creating a constant influx of new recruits. Consequently, the need for continuous training incurred substantial costs, resembling a "leaking bucket" scenario.

More

To address this challenge and boost customer satisfaction, Airtel initiated a project utilizing the Six Sigma approach. The project revolved around optimizing the "3Ps": people, processes, and policies. A comprehensive Fish Bone analysis unearthed the root causes behind the persistently high staff attrition rates. Through the project's implementation, Airtel not only curtailed attrition significantly but also introduced a structured Career Path program for ARC staff, providing them with a clear trajectory for professional growth. Furthermore, the project realigned processes and policies to harmonize with the new workforce dynamics.

The profound impact of this endeavor was reflected in the CSat scores, which witnessed a remarkable 12% improvement on a quarterly basis. Recognizing the success and transformative potential of this project, Airtel chose to replicate the model across its operations in India. This achievement underscores Airtel's commitment to innovation, customer-centricity, and continual improvement in delivering telecommunications services to millions of customers across the nation.
