  • TALENT MANAGEMENT

    • Talent management refers to the strategic process of attracting, developing, retaining, and optimizing an organization's workforce to achieve its business goals. It encompasses a range of activities designed to identify, nurture, and utilize the skills, abilities, and potential of employees in a way that benefits both the individual and the organization as a whole. Talent management goes beyond traditional human resource management by focusing on a more comprehensive and strategic approach to workforce management. Talent Management as a whole includes Talent Acquisition, Onboarding, Performance Management, Learning and Development, Succession Planning, Employee Engagement, Diversity and Inclusion and Employee well-being.


  • PERFORMANCE MANAGEMENT

    • Performance management is a comprehensive process that involves planning, monitoring, evaluating, and improving an employee's performance and contributions within an organization. It's a systematic approach to aligning individual and team goals with the organization's strategic objectives. The primary goal of performance management is to enhance employee effectiveness, productivity, and job satisfaction while achieving desired business outcomes. The overall process may involve Goal Setting, Performance Feedback, Performance Feedback, Identifying strengths and areas of improvement, Coaching for Performance, Training and Development, Career Pathing etc.


  • LEARNING AND DEVELOPMENT

    • Learning and development (L&D) is the process of enhancing the skills, knowledge, competencies, and capabilities of employees within an organization. It involves planned activities and initiatives designed to improve individual and collective performance, support career growth, and contribute to the achievement of organizational goals. Learning and development strategies are vital for maintaining a competitive edge in today's rapidly changing business landscape.
    An effective learning and development strategy contributes to higher employee engagement, improved job satisfaction, better employee retention, and enhanced organizational performance. By investing in the growth of employees' skills and knowledge, organizations can adapt to industry changes, innovate, and remain competitive.

  • EMPLOYEE ENGAGEMENT

    • Employee engagement refers to the emotional commitment, dedication, and involvement that employees have toward their work, their organization, and its goals. Engaged employees are enthusiastic about their roles, feel a sense of ownership over their work, and are motivated to contribute their best efforts. Employee engagement goes beyond job satisfaction; it reflects a deeper connection between employees and their workplace. A high Employee Engagement results in Higher employee productivity, higher retention, higher customer satisfaction, higher employer branding and higher productivity and revenue therefore. We help you devise effective Employee Engagement Strategies to make a long lasting business impact

  • COMPETENCY ASSESSMENT & DEVELOPMENT

    • Competency development and assessment is a systematic process aimed at identifying, building, and evaluating the skills, knowledge, behaviors, and attributes that employees need to perform their roles effectively. Competencies are specific, observable, and measurable traits that contribute to individual and organizational success. Developing and assessing competencies is crucial for aligning employees' capabilities with business objectives and ensuring optimal performance. Overall, competency development and assessment are integral components of talent management, ensuring that employees have the skills needed to drive organizational success and adapt to changing business needs.

  • LEADERSHIP DEVELOPMENT

    • Leadership development is the process of enhancing the skills, knowledge, behaviors, and qualities of individuals to prepare them for leadership roles within an organization. It involves systematic efforts to identify, nurture, and groom potential leaders, equipping them with the capabilities required to guide teams, make strategic decisions, and drive organizational success. Effective leadership development programs contribute to building a pipeline of skilled and competent leaders who can inspire and motivate their teams to achieve goals. The Leadership development strategies could include Training Programs, Coaching and Mentoring, 360 Degree Feedback, Project Based Leaning etc. An effective Leadership Development strategy solves the problem of Leadership Pipeline for the organization and future leaders are made available from the existing talent pool itself.

  • COACHING FOR PERFORMANCE

    • Coaching for performance involves a structured and collaborative process where a coach works closely with an employee to enhance their skills, improve their performance, and achieve specific goals. The goal of coaching is to help employees reach their full potential, address challenges, and excel in their roles. Effective coaching goes beyond giving instructions; it focuses on guiding individuals to discover their strengths, identify areas for improvement, and develop strategies for success. Coaching for performance requires active listening, empathy, patience, and a commitment to supporting employees' growth and success. It's a valuable tool for unlocking potential and driving positive outcomes in the workplace.

    The problem with many organizations worldwide is that the exepcted outcome of a job one is doing is not clearly defined. Result is that one ends up being appraised on something at the end of the year of which neither he was made aware of in the beginning of the year nor during the entire year worst is that most of the time the appraisal is on many subjective KRAs which are extremely difficult to measure. Media being no exception, had similar kind of issues where the managers rating their subordinates on either subjective KRAs or no clear defined KRAs leaving enough ambiguity and heartburn thereafter. We embarked upon the journey of Gallup Q12 first Question which states 'I know what is expected of me at work' and put Objectivity in KRAs of all the roles resulting into something which can be measured and is data based. The role holder himself knows his performance clearly during the entire year thereby relieving him of a false sense of achievement. It becomes easier for manager also to do performance appraisal since data speaks for itself. This helps in a better expectation management during appraisals and less heartburns during and after the process. Subsequently a Job Directory was created where each role, its purpose, Job Family and KRAs are mentioned which acts as a bible for recruiters and HR team across the organization. Parallely a Manpower and Salary Benchmarking study for a National Hindi News Channel and a Regional Channel was also created for Times to be used for all hiring decisions.
